# PHASE 2: Data Diversity Investigation
# Multi-Dataset Training (FaceForensics++ + CelebDF)

## Objective

Evaluate whether increasing training data diversity improves deepfake detection generalization more effectively than changing model architecture.

The baseline model (EXP_002) was trained only on FaceForensics++ and achieved strong in-dataset performance but poor cross-dataset performance on CelebDF.

This experiment investigates whether combining FaceForensics++ and CelebDF during training improves robustness.

---

## Model

* Architecture: EfficientNet-B0
* Pretrained Weights: ImageNet
* Output Classes: Real / Fake

---

### Improvement over EXP_002
FaceForensics++:

* Accuracy: +10.29%

CelebDF:

* Accuracy: +31.58%
* F1 Score: +26.18%

### Key Observation

Multi-dataset training improved performance on both FaceForensics++ and CelebDF simultaneously.

No performance degradation was observed on FaceForensics++ despite introducing a second training dataset. Performance improved substantially on both FaceForensics++ and CelebDF.

These results suggest that training data diversity was a more significant bottleneck than model architecture in earlier experiments.

---

## Key Findings

1. Training data diversity had a dramatic impact on performance.

2. The major limitation of EXP_002 was dataset bias rather than model capacity.

3. EfficientNet-B0 was capable of learning generalized deepfake features when exposed to multiple datasets.

4. Multi-dataset training produced a substantially more robust detector than single-dataset training.

---

## Conclusion
The results indicate that training data diversity was a more significant limiting factor than model architecture for EfficientNet-B0 in this project.

By incorporating multiple deepfake datasets during training, the model achieved strong performance on both FaceForensics++ and CelebDF without sacrificing accuracy on either dataset.

This experiment establishes a significantly stronger baseline for future robustness evaluations on WildDeepfake, DeeperForensics, and other real-world datasets.

Future work should focus on evaluating robustness against real-world datasets such as:

* WildDeepfake
* DeeperForensics
* DFDC

before investigating alternative architectures such as ConvNeXt-Tiny.
