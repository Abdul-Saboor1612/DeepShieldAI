# PHASE 2: Data Diversity Investigation
# Multi-Dataset Training (FaceForensics++ + CelebDF)

## Objective

Evaluate whether increasing training data diversity improves deepfake detection generalization more effectively than changing model architecture.

The baseline model (EXP_002) was trained only on FaceForensics++ and achieved strong in-dataset performance but poor cross-dataset performance on CelebDF.

This experiment investigates whether combining FaceForensics++ and CelebDF during training improves robustness.

---

## Model

* Architecture: EfficientNet-B0
* Pretrained Weights: ImageNet
* Output Classes: Real / Fake

---

* Real Images: 50,000

## WildDeepfake Evaluation (Unseen Dataset)

### Objective

Evaluate the robustness of EXP_007 on a completely unseen real-world deepfake dataset.

Unlike FaceForensics++ and CelebDF, WildDeepfake contains deepfakes collected from the internet and includes a wider variety of compression artifacts, image quality variations, lighting conditions, and manipulation techniques.

The dataset was not used during training.

---

### Evaluation Dataset

WildDeepfake Test Set

Dataset Size:

* Real Images: 3370
* Fake Images: 3398
* Total Images: 6768

---

### Results

Accuracy: 63.24%
F1 Score: 55.32%

Confusion Matrix:
[[2740, 630],
[1858, 1540]]

Classification Report:

Real:
* Precision: 0.60
* Recall: 0.81
* F1: 0.69

Fake:
* Precision: 0.71
* Recall: 0.45
* F1: 0.55

---

### Analysisn of test on unseen dataset

The model maintained reasonable performance on real images but struggled to correctly identify many fake samples.

A significant number of fake images were incorrectly classified as real:

* False Negatives: 1858

This resulted in a fake-image recall of only 45%.

The findings suggest that although multi-dataset training substantially improved performance on FaceForensics++ and CelebDF, the model still has difficulty generalizing to real-world deepfake distributions that differ from those seen during training.

---

### Key Observation

Training on FaceForensics++ and CelebDF alone was insufficient to achieve strong real-world robustness.

While the model generalized effectively between benchmark datasets, performance dropped significantly on WildDeepfake, indicating that additional training diversity is required.

This experiment highlights the gap between benchmark performance and real-world deployment performance.

---

### Conclusion

WildDeepfake revealed a major limitation of EXP_007.

The model learned benchmark-specific deepfake characteristics effectively but did not generalize well to internet-sourced deepfakes.

Future work should focus on incorporating real-world datasets such as:

* WildDeepfake
* DeeperForensics
* DFDC

into the training pipeline to improve robustness against unseen manipulations and real-world image conditions.
